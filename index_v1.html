About Triangle Calculations

This calculator determines the missing sides and angles of a triangle. You must provide exactly 3 pieces of information, including at least one side.

  • All sides must be in the same unit (e.g., all in meters or all in feet).
  • In a valid solution, all side lengths are positive, and all angles are between 0° and 180°.
  • 3 sides (SSS) yields a unique solution (or no solution if the longest side exceeds the sum of the other sides).
  • 2 angles and a side (ASA) yields a unique solution if the angles sum to less than 180°.
  • 2 sides and an enclosed angle (SAS) yields a unique solution.
  • 2 sides and a non-enclosed angle (SSA) may yield 0, 1, or 2 solutions.

Tip: Click on a letter in the triangle diagram to jump to that input field.

About Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation has the form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a, b, and c are constants and a ≠ 0.

The solutions are given by the quadratic formula:

x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / (2a)

Where:

  • If b² - 4ac > 0, there are two distinct real solutions
  • If b² - 4ac = 0, there is one repeated real solution
  • If b² - 4ac < 0, there are two complex solutions
